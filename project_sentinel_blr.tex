\section{Executive Summary}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\textbf{Sentinel-BLR is not a traffic violation detector. It is an evidence-quality system — one that determines when a detected violation is legally reliable enough for enforcement.}

Most submissions for this problem will bolt together YOLO, OCR, and a violation classifier. Sentinel-BLR does that too — and then solves the three problems that make those pipelines fail in actual Bengaluru deployment:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.8em, itemsep=0.3em]
    \item \textbf{Cameras that drift} — addressed by a self-healing Auto-Calibration Heartbeat (SIFT/ORB + inverse homography $H^{-1}$) detecting and correcting spatial drift every 15 minutes, without halting enforcement.
    \item \textbf{Plates that are unreadable} — addressed by Temporal Character-Level Probability Fusion across 5 frames with Levenshtein outlier filtering, producing a majority-vote plate string even from mud-damaged plates.
    \item \textbf{Detection confidence that does not translate into legally defensible enforcement} — addressed by per-violation-type confidence thresholds, a configuration-file policy engine (not an ML model), and a composite Evidence Reliability Score (ERS) on every evidence packet.
\end{itemize}

Sentinel-BLR processes live camera feeds under real-world environmental stress — night, rain, motion blur, monsoon fog, sodium vapour streetlighting — and detects all seven categories of traffic violation defined in the problem statement.

\subsection*{Four Architectural Principles}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.8em, itemsep=0.2em]
    \item Detect violations reliably under adverse Bengaluru conditions.
    \item Gate enforcement on evidence quality, not raw detection confidence.
    \item Keep all enforcement policy inspectable and updateable without model redeployment.
    \item Produce legally defensible, tamper-evident evidence packets on every confirmed violation.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Problem Statement and Gap Analysis}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection*{Why Manual Enforcement Fails}

Bengaluru's road network spans approximately 14,000~km. The BTP's CCTV network generates far more footage than any human team can review in real time. Manual enforcement has three structural failure modes:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.8em, itemsep=0.2em]
    \item \textbf{Scale} — more camera-hours than human officer capacity.
    \item \textbf{Environmental} — degraded performance in low-light, rain, and haze.
    \item \textbf{Strategic} — deployment based on officer intuition rather than violation density evidence.
\end{itemize}

Sentinel-BLR closes all three.

\section{System Uniqueness and Competitive Differentiation}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection*{KEY DIFFERENTIATOR: Adaptive Temporal Striding (ATS)}

In adverse weather, rather than suppressing sub-models (which reduces detection coverage) or running every sub-model (which blows the budget), Layer 2A applies ATS: classification runs on every 5th frame for affected tracks. A vehicle remains in the ROI for $\sim$30--60 frames, yielding 6--12 classification samples — sufficient for high-confidence multi-frame consensus while staying within the 40~ms per-frame budget. ATS replaces suppression as the graceful degradation strategy.

\subsection*{KEY DIFFERENTIATOR: Computationally Honest at Every Layer}

v4.2 is the first version of Sentinel-BLR that can be honestly presented as real-time on real hardware. The combination of TensorRT INT8, trigger-gated sub-model activation, per-Track-ID inference gating, ATS, and async queues produces a design-target cycle of 28~ms typical / 38~ms peak on Jetson Orin NX — within the 40~ms budget for 25~fps. End-to-end p95 $\leq 38$~ms is the Phase 1 measured validation target; the design target is not presented as a measured benchmark.

\subsection*{KEY DIFFERENTIATOR: Self-Healing Spatial Geometry}

Auto-Calibration Heartbeat is absent from virtually every ANPR or CV traffic enforcement system currently deployed in India. Camera drift is not hypothetical; it is routine. Without auto-calibration, a virtual stop-line polygon can drift 1--3 metres of projected ground distance per degree of camera tilt — silently producing false positives or missed violations. Sentinel-BLR detects drift within 15 minutes, re-projects all spatial ROIs automatically, and notifies operators without halting enforcement.

\subsection*{KEY DIFFERENTIATOR: Temporal Character-Level Probability Fusion}

Standard multi-frame OCR voting takes the highest-confidence full string across frames. Sentinel-BLR's approach is fundamentally different: it stores softmax probability vectors for each character position independently across 5 frames, sums the probability matrices, and applies argmax per character position. This means a character that is clearly visible in 2 frames but occluded in 3 still contributes its probability mass — allowing partial-visibility frames to contribute to a complete plate read. Levenshtein distance is used only as an outlier filter to discard corrupt frames before fusion, not as the voting mechanism.

\subsection*{KEY DIFFERENTIATOR: Hybrid Evidence Payloads over Kafka}

The architectural tension: human reviewers need to see the violation frame, but streaming raw video over Kafka at multi-junction scale is network-prohibitive. Sentinel-BLR resolves this with Hybrid Evidence Payloads: each Kafka message carries full JSON metadata plus a Base64-encoded compressed JPEG crop of the violation ROI ($\sim$400$\times$400 pixels, $<$50~KB). Reviewers see the violation without full-frame streaming.

\subsection*{KEY DIFFERENTIATOR: Policy Engine as Configuration, Not ML}

The enforcement policy engine (Layer 4A) is a YAML configuration file. BTP can update enforcement rules — add a diversion polygon, raise the OCR confidence floor, extend a warning-only phase for a new junction, suspend auto-fining for a violation type under legal review — without redeploying any model. Changes take effect in under a minute. The enforcement decision-making logic is inspectable, auditable, and modifiable by non-engineers.

\subsection*{KEY DIFFERENTIATOR: Evidence Reliability Score (ERS)}

Every evidence packet carries a single composite integer (0--100) distilling detection confidence, OCR quality, frame consensus depth, calibration health, and preprocessing quality into one number that officers, reviewers, and courts read at a glance. The ERS formula is:

\begin{equation}
\text{ERS} = (0.30 \times \text{detection\_confidence}) + (0.25 \times \text{OCR\_confidence}) + (0.20 \times \text{frame\_consensus\_ratio}) + (0.15 \times \text{calibration\_health}) + (0.10 \times \text{preprocessing\_quality})
\end{equation}

No new model is required; all components are already computed upstream.

\subsection*{KEY DIFFERENTIATOR: Closed-Loop Strategic Intelligence}

Violations detected today update the junction heatmap tonight, which updates deployment recommendations tomorrow. The recency-decay formula ($0.95^{\,\text{days\_ago}}$) rapidly shifts weight from historical baselines to live data. This self-improving cycle is not present in documented single-violation ANPR deployments in Indian cities.

Under extreme conditions (all 4 preprocessing stages active), Layer 2A applies Adaptive Temporal Striding: classification sub-models process every 5th frame for tracked vehicles rather than every frame. ATS replaces sub-model suppression as the graceful degradation strategy, maintaining detection coverage while managing compute.

\subsection*{Layer 1A — Auto-Calibration Heartbeat}

Every 15 minutes, the Auto-Calibration Heartbeat runs as a background process independent of the main inference pipeline. Without auto-calibration, a virtual stop-line polygon can drift 1--3 metres of projected ground distance per degree of camera tilt — enough to falsely flag compliant vehicles or miss genuine violations.

\textbf{Mathematical grounding:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.8em, itemsep=0.2em]
    \item \textbf{Installation:} A Calibration Reference Frame is generated — 40--60 stable visual landmarks (kerb edges, road markings, signal poles, wall corners) extracted via SIFT/ORB feature detection, stored with projected ground-plane coordinates.
    \item \textbf{Every 15~min:} Heartbeat extracts the same landmark set from the live frame and computes homography matrix $H$ between current and reference positions.
    \item \textbf{Drift threshold:} If $H$ shows translation or rotation $> 1.2$~px at 1080p ($\sim$0.08° tilt), a Calibration Drift Alert is raised.
    \item \textbf{On alert:} All spatial ROIs (stop-line polygons, no-parking zones, Safe-Flow Vectors) are automatically re-projected using inverse homography: $\mathbf{p}' = H^{-1}\mathbf{p}$. Enforcement continues without interruption.
    \item \textbf{Degraded mode:} If landmark match rate falls below 50\%, the junction is flagged \texttt{Calibration-Uncertain} and all detections route to human review.
    \item \textbf{Guard condition:} Scene occupancy monitor delays heartbeat when heavy vehicle coverage $> 50\%$ of frame; retries within 60 seconds. Maximum drift-window cap: if calibration cannot be obtained within 45 minutes of the scheduled heartbeat, the junction enters \texttt{Calibration-Uncertain} mode.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Layer 3 — Violation Detection Engine: Five Parallel Paths}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.8em, itemsep=0.4em]
    \item \textbf{Path A — Helmet / Seatbelt:} MobileNetV3 binary classifier (triggered only on two-wheeler or car bounding box from Layer 2). Initialized from IDD + ACFR + Roboflow Helmet pre-trained weights. 3-frame consensus required before escalation to Layer 4.

    \item \textbf{Path B — Triple Riding (Hybrid Module):}
    \begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=0.2em]
        \item \textit{Primary method (high-density, crowd index $> 0.35$):} Volumetric Bounding-Box Profiling — aspect ratio and pixel density of the motorcycle bounding box are analysed; flag raised if vertical/horizontal mass distribution exceeds $\Delta \geq 1.2\times$ single-rider baseline. 3-frame consensus required.
        \item \textit{Precision-enhancement method (low-to-medium density, crowd index $\leq 0.35$):} Chassis-Person Centroid Association with velocity coupling. MediaPipe Pose Lite extracts torso centroids within the expanded two-wheeler bounding box. Only centroids whose velocity vector is within 15° of vehicle heading and 0.5~m/s magnitude tolerance are counted as riders. 3-frame consensus required.
        \item Disagreement between both active paths routes to human review.
    \end{itemize}

    \item \textbf{Path C — Wrong-Side Driving:} Safe-Flow Vector maps generated at calibration via SIFT/ORB auto-estimation from OSM road geometry. Deviation angle threshold: 25° default, adjustable per junction. Two-frame sustain requirement. Minimum velocity filter eliminates stationary U-turns.

    \item \textbf{Path D — Stop-Line and Red-Light Crossing:} Virtual stop-line polygons defined at calibration. Signal state determined by a YOLO lamp-colour classifier (primary operative method — trained to classify signal head crop as RED/AMBER/GREEN, robust to sodium-vapour lighting and rain). BTP Traffic Signal API integration is a Phase 2 aspirational enhancement. Violation fires on simultaneous centroid-in-ROI and classifier-RED conditions.

    \item \textbf{Path E — Illegal Parking:} Stationary-threshold filter with 180-second duration (configurable per zone type). No-parking ROI sources: manual annotation, BMTC GTFS bus-stop polygons, Dataset B parking-concentration zones. Velocity floor: 0.05~m/s to exclude red-light stops.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Layer 5 — License Plate OCR with Temporal Probability Fusion}

Standard multi-frame OCR voting selects the highest-confidence full string across frames. Sentinel-BLR's approach is fundamentally different:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.8em, itemsep=0.2em]
    \item \textbf{Per-character softmax storage:} Softmax probability vectors for each character position are stored independently across up to 5 frames, chosen for maximum plate visibility (bounding box aspect ratio).
    \item \textbf{Matrix summation:} Probability matrices are summed element-wise across all frames. Characters clearly visible in 2 frames but partially occluded in 3 still contribute their probability mass.
    \item \textbf{Argmax per position:} Final plate string is the argmax of the summed matrix per character position — yielding the highest-probability character at each position across all frames.
    \item \textbf{Levenshtein outlier filter:} Applied before fusion to discard corrupt frames (e.g. mud splash obscuring all characters). Edit distance threshold filters outliers; this is not the voting mechanism.
    \item \textbf{Majority-vote confirmation:} If majority string exists (at least 3/5 frames agree within edit distance 1), that string is the confirmed plate read. If not, LOW\_CONFIDENCE flag routes to human review.
\end{itemize}

Levenshtein correction pairs for Indian number-plate font: 0/O, 1/I, 8/B, S/5. KA-regex gate validates Karnataka plate format before indexing.

\subsection*{Layer 6 — Evidence Packet + Evidence Reliability Score (ERS)}

Each confirmed violation generates a tamper-evident evidence packet containing: annotated frame with GradCAM overlay, UTC timestamp, GPS coordinates, junction identifier, plate text with confidence, violation type and offence code, vehicle class, review metadata, SHA-256 hash of annotated frame bytes, and ERS (0--100).

\begin{equation}
\text{ERS} = (0.30 \times \text{detection\_confidence}) + (0.25 \times \text{OCR\_confidence}) + (0.20 \times \text{frame\_consensus\_ratio}) + (0.15 \times \text{calibration\_health}) + (0.10 \times \text{preprocessing\_quality})
\end{equation}

ERS routing: $\geq 85$ — auto-fine eligible; $60$--$84$ — human review queue; $< 60$ — discarded. The SHA-256 hash makes any subsequent modification to the annotated frame detectable in legal proceedings.

\subsection*{Active Learning Feedback Loop}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.8em, itemsep=0.2em]
    \item \textbf{Label generation:} Every human accept/reject in the 60--92\% confidence band is recorded as a labelled training example (image crop, ground-truth label, reviewer ID, timestamp) in a dedicated fine-tuning queue.
    \item \textbf{Weekly LoRA fine-tuning:} Sunday 02:00 maintenance window applies Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) updates to MobileNetV3. LoRA restricts parameter updates to low-rank decomposition matrices, preventing catastrophic forgetting while adapting to local Bengaluru conditions at minimal compute cost.
    \item \textbf{Model drift detection:} Rolling reviewer-override rate on the most recent 500 labelled examples is monitored in Layer 9 dashboard. Rising override rate triggers ahead-of-schedule fine-tuning.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Sentinel-BLR is an evidence-quality system designed to survive contact with the real world — the real Bengaluru, with cameras that shift in the wind, monsoon rain that obscures plates, markets where three bikes ride side by side at two metres per second, and an enforcement environment where an automated fine issued on low-reliability evidence is a legal liability, not a civic service.

The architectural choices documented here — Adaptive Temporal Striding, crowd-density-gated Volumetric Bounding-Box Profiling, Temporal Character-Level Probability Fusion, Hybrid Evidence Payloads, auto-calibration heartbeat, confidence-separated enforcement policy, legal grounding in MV Act 2019, and a graduated enforcement tier that builds public trust before applying fines — each exist because a specific, credible failure mode demanded them.

The result is a system that is technically proficient, operationally honest, legally defensible, and incrementally deployable — starting at ten high-urgency junctions identified from Dataset B analysis, improving every night it runs, and scaling to city-wide deployment without architectural replacement.
